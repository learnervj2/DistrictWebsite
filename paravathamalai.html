Jamunamarathur Hill

Parvathamalai is situated in the Tiruvannamalai district of Tamilnadu. It is located at a distance of 40kms from Tiruvannamalai and 20 kms from Polur. It is almost 4500 feet above mean sea level and it can be accessed only by foot. The total trek distance is 9 kms.

Why is it so special about this hill?

It is believed that when Hanuman was carrying the Sanjeevi hill to Lanka, pieces from it scattered and fell in many places. Parvathamalai is one such place. Therefore, the breeze from this hill is highly medicinal and can cure one of many ailments.

Another interesting fact about this hill is that the Lingam (Mallikarjuna Swamy) on top of the hill was installed by Bogar, who is one among the 18 Siddhas.

The trek to reach Parvathamalai temple is a unique one. The trail consists of plains, steps, uphill rocky path, crowbar path(Kadaparai padhai), ladder steps(Yeni Padhai),track steps(Thandavala Padi) and sky steps(Agaya Padhai).

Thenmathimangalam (Thenmahadevamangalam) is the gateway to Parvathamalai Thenmadhimangalam is a sleepy yet serene village in the Kadaladi hill range. We could see the towering peak of the Parvathamalai hills at a distance from this village.

One actually needs to cross six hills to reach the top of the 7th hill where the temple is actually located. The six hills are Kadaladi malai, Kanakachi Odai malai, Methaga malai, Kadaparai malai, Kumari nettu malai and Puttru malai.

How to Reach:

By Air

Nearest Air Port is Chennai which around 189 Kilometers away from Tiruvannamalai.

By Train

Name of nearest railway station POLUR. This station POLUR is in between Tiruvannamalai to Vellore. POLUR is situated around 40 kilometers from Tiruvannamalai.

By Road
